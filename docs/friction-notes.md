# Friction notes

Running log of real moments of friction while actually brewing with MeadOS —
not a feature list, not a design doc. The point is to only act on things that
show up here from real use, instead of guessing from reading code.

How to use it: whenever something makes you pause, hesitate, re-read, or
think "why didn't it just tell me X" — add a line. Don't overthink the
format. A `?` in the "fix?" column just means "not sure yet, revisit later."

| Date | What happened | What I expected instead | Fix? |
|------|----------------|--------------------------|------|
|      |                |                          |      |

After a few weeks: look for repeats. One mention of something is noise.
Three or four independent mentions of the same page/section is signal —
that's what turns into the next real piece of work.

Also worth noting, per the same practice: things you *never* look at or
always scroll past. Ignored-but-present is as useful a signal as friction.
